Chronic Constipation: The Leading Cause of Piles

Understanding how physical damage happens inside the body
  • What are Piles? Swollen, permanently inflamed blood vessels inside or around your rectum and anus.
  • The Main Trigger: Heavy, forceful physical straining during bowel movements to pass hard stool packs.
  • The Role of Hydration: Your large intestine requires constant water intake to maintain a smooth, soft stool consistency.
  • The Failure Cycle: If the body lacks fluid, it sucks water directly out of your digestive waste, leaving it rocky and dry.

The "Heavy Water" Trap: Why High TDS Lowers Your Thirst

How hard mineral water directly tricks your body's nerve systems
  • Bitter and Metallic Taste: Heavy groundwater loaded with calcium and magnesium possesses an unappealing, chalky flavor.
  • Suppressed Thirst Response: Because the water tastes bad, your brain subconsciously delays your drinking urge. You only drink when parched.
  • False Fullness Sensation: Dense mineral water sits heavily in your stomach, falsely signaling that you are full before you are genuinely hydrated.
  • Internal Water Drainage: Highly dense salt solutions draw fluid away from your bowel tract to process through the kidneys.

Action Plan for Healthy Hydration & Gut Relief

Simple daily adjustments to protect your rectal health
  • Test Your Drinking Water: Ensure your final drinking water TDS rests comfortably between 50 to 150 ppm.
  • Check Purification Setup: Use an RO (Reverse Osmosis) unit equipped with a properly calibrated TDS controller. Standard UV filters cannot remove hardness.
  • Enforce a Fluid Target: Actively consume 2.5 to 3 Liters of lightweight water daily. Do not wait until your throat feels dry.
  • Monitor Your Body: Check your urine shade routinely. Clear or light straw-color means safe. Dark yellow indicates a high risk for piles.
